## Design Context

### Users

University of Oregon students navigating course selection. They're in a high-stakes academic context, trying to make informed decisions about professors and courses that will impact their GPA and academic success. They come to DuckGrades during stressful periods (registration, schedule planning) and need reliable data quickly, but they also appreciate tools that don't feel sterile or corporate.

### Brand Personality

**Friendly, Playful, Smart**

- **Friendly**: Approachable and welcoming, not intimidating
- **Playful**: Subtle duck/oregon nature references and personality touches, but never cartoonish or obviously playful
- **Smart**: Data-forward, trustworthy, helps students make intelligent decisions

The interface should feel like a helpful classmate who knows all the grade data — not a boring administrative tool, not a SaaS product, not a corporate dashboard.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Fun and memorable, not corporate**

- Avoid SaaS landing page aesthetics (no generic hero gradients, no "modern corporate" minimalism)
- Avoid boring academic tool stereotypes
- Embrace the existing nature-inspired sage green palette (already well-established)
- Keep the professional-minimal foundation from docs/design.md, but infuse it with subtle moments of delight
- The data is the hero — presentation should enhance comprehension, not distract from it

**Theme**: Light/dark/system toggle already implemented — maintain this flexibility

### Design Principles

1. **Data-First Delight**: The joy comes from clarity and ease of use, not decoration. Make complex grade data instantly comprehensible and satisfying to explore.

2. **Subtle Playfulness**: Use the duck brand and nature elements sparingly but memorably. A touch of personality goes a long way — avoid overplaying the mascot.

3. **Speed Over Flash**: Students need answers fast. Prioritize performance, quick navigation, and instant feedback over elaborate animations or transitions.

4. **Trust Through Transparency**: Present data honestly and clearly. No dark patterns, no hiding complexity — let students see exactly what they're getting.

5. **Break the Academic Mold**: This isn't a boring university portal. Make it feel special and worth coming back to, even when users aren't actively researching courses.

### Accessibility Notes

- Follow standard WCAG practices
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for animations
- Maintain existing focus ring and keyboard navigation patterns
- No specific accessibility requirements beyond standard web best practices
